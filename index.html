The Eiffel Tower

Constructed in 1889 as the enterance of the World's Fair the Eiffel Tower is a world icon for France sitting in the center of Paris. Engineer Gustave Eiffel designed it as a wrought iron lattice tower and it was initially criticized for its design. It has turned out to be the most visited paid monument in the world with about 7 million visitors.

Standing at 1,063 ft tall it is by far the tallest structure in Paris. Over the years, the Eiffel Tower has been the site of numerous high-profile stunts, ceremonial events and even scientific experiments. In 1911, for instance, the German physicist Theodor Wulf used an electrometer to detect higher levels of radiation at its top than at its base, observing the effects of what are now called cosmic rays. The Eiffel Tower has also inspired more than 30 replicas and similar structures in various cities around the world.
